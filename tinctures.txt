Making tinctures: base recipe
Look at allowed % of the herb; i.e. a ratio of 1:5 in recipes means 20% or 200 grams per liter ethanol
All material (leaves, flowers, bark, ...) needs to be cut finely.
The ethanol to be used should have an ethanol content of 70% to 80% and is preferably relatively flavorless and inexpensive (i.e. 70-80% vodka). Let the herb sit in the ethanol for 10 days, then strain off the herb from the liquid. Store the liquid in dark bottles. Can be used upto at least 2 years.
For use, respect the amount of drops (each drop will be about 0,05 ml) or just measure off using a measuring cup (ml).

Hypericum perforatum (depression):
200 grams of flowering tips, crushed flowers (flos) on 1 liter of ethanol
3x 30 drops per day (=4,5 ml)
Recipe based on: Groot Handboek Geneeskrachtige Planten by Geert Verhelst

Fraxinus excelsior (detox):

Echinacea purpurea (immune system):
200 grams of all above-ground plant parts (herba) on 1 liter of ethanol
3x 20 drops per day (=3ml), use tincture daily over a period of 3 weeks
